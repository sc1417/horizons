Stop and Lock

A simple, flexible, temporary solution for your privacy in shared accomodation.

About Us

Bob the Builder is a company founded by five Imperial College students who are frustrated by not being able to lock their doors in a shared accomodation. The aim of the Stop and Lock project is to create a simple, removable and errgonomic solution to improve living conditions and reduccing problems like theft and intrusions within shared accomodation.

With a simple touch, you would be able to lock your door from both sides and would not need to worry about your stuff getting stolen or people barging into your room! The lock would be scratch- and water-proof,and would have anti-thief alarms installed. It would also be extremely easy to set up, and would just clip on to any door that exists. GET HYPED

Good Websites

In my opinion, a good website must have a simple design that leads the targeted audience straight towards what they are expecting from the website. It would also need to be trustworthy and safe, meaning that there should not be an overload and adds and phishing pop-up windows. In addition, in a mobile device-oriented world like today’s, a good website should be able to change its presentation accordingly to the dimensions of the users’ device screens.

Accessibility

Web accessibility is the practice of removing barriers from websites in a way that people with situational and physical disabilities, and people with socio-economic restrictions on bandwidth speed would be able to access them and retrieve the same amount of information from them as a normal person would do. Considerations include underlined links for colour blind people, large buttons for people who cannot control a mouse, close captions for people with oral impairments, and self-explaining illustrations for people with dyslexia or other learning disabilities.

GDPR and Privacy Concerns

GDPR is an acronym for the “General Data Protection Regulation” implemented by the European Union parliament in 2018, aiming to give people control of their personal data. Upon the implementation of the regulation, sites would have to inform their users of the presence of any form of data collection and the users will have to consent for the sites to let them proceed. The users would have the right to request for a copy for the data that the side has collected for other purposes (commercial mostly) at any time. Pseudonymization or anonymization is essential so that no real identity should be directly linked to any set of data. Businesses would have to report data breaches within 72 hours if they have adverse effects on the users’ privacy. For my website, since it is technically used for a business purpose, I would have to request consent from my audience for access of their IP addresses, names and emails from the contacts section and cookies for marketing purposes. This would be implemented either by a pop-up window whilst the user tries to access my website and a checkbox at the end of the contacts section requiring them to read a set of terms and conditions.

Website Marketing Strategies

First, I would try to make use of cookies and spread the information in the form of ads within social media. Secondly, I would set up a social media page on Facebook or Instagram to spread the “coolness” of the product with aesthetic content and promotional videos. Third, I would try to negotiate with major furniture retailers and supermarkets so that when there would be a QR code on the side of my product that links to my website to provide potential customers with further information about the product.

Next Steps

I would try to build a reputation for my product from sales within e-commerce giants including amazon, potentially creating a custom theme for our product tailored to their product page or linking their site to ours. Since students are the main targeted customers, I would consider the idea of setting up a student discount and advertising it on platforms where students would be most interested such as Unidays and StudentBeans. This information would be shown in bold right under the menu section so that people viewing the website would be able to see it all the time. As the company expands to the point where we would not need to rely on amazon’s supply chain, an e-commerce portal would be set up to maximise the profit from retail sales. Again, customers would have access to discount codes from purchasing from the website instead of amazon by subscribing to our automated e-flyer system. Beyond that, people would receive email about new versions of the product and sales information and that would subconsciously drive them to buy our products.
